Life

The discovery of the woods

INTRODUCTION

Being my first time in Ireland, I can't help but be fascinated by all the different and beautiful shades of green that now, in Autumn, turn yellow and red.
I have always loved nature and all the things related to it: the smell of wood, the color of the leaves, the sound of all the small creatures that inhabit the forest.
Here, walking along the Shannon River, I made some small discoveries worthy of my attention and appreciation that reminded me that it is possible to find life even in the smallest particle.

Swan line
The Living Bridge is an interesting construction that rises above the river Shannon.
It’s the best spot to appreciate the swans dance begging for food, turning around and plunging their head in the water.

Apple light
Autumn is not only the season where all plants die to survive the Winter, but it is also the season where some plants turn their flowers into delicious fruits such as this apple tree. This season is not about death, on the contrary, it is about life.

Revolving cone
It is really interesting how nature embraces itself, in the truest sense of the word: the branches of the tree, which support the pine cones, revolve around the main one, squeezing it in a soft grip.

Mushroom tongues
Sometimes, when we go deep into the woods, the plants we encounter seem to be surrounded by a different atmosphere.
It will be the light, or the fact that we see them from a different perspective, in either way they almost seem to come alive.

Swan harmony
A small corner where nature seems to embrace the swan, as a house would embrace its guest.
The reflection on the water, the general atmosphere and the quiet of the animal make me think about how peaceful and at home he feels, just living.
